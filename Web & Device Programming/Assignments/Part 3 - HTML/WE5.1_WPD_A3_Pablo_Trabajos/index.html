Triangle

A triangle is a polygon with three edges and three vertices. It is one of the basic shapes in geometry.

  • Edges: 4
  • Schläfli symbol: {3} (for equilateral)
  • Internal angle: 60º

Square

In geometry, a square is a regular quadrilateral, which means that it has four equal sides and four equal angles (90-degree angles, or right angles). It can also be defined as a rectangle in which two adjacent sides have equal length.

  • Edges: 4
  • Schläfli symbol: {4}
  • Internal angle: 90º
See more

Pentagon

In geometry, a pentagon (from the Greek πέντε pente and γωνία gonia, meaning five and angle) is any five-sided polygon or 5-gon. The sum of the internal angles in a simple pentagon is 540°. A pentagon may be simple or self-intersecting. A self-intersecting regular pentagon (or star pentagon) is called a pentagram.

  • Edges: 5
  • Schläfli symbol: {5}
  • Internal angle: 108º
